## Tier A — sub_type classification (free)

Run: `python3 -m qa.run_qa --tier A` → 21/21.

Two prompts initially flagged a mismatch; both were corpus-design errors, confirmed by codex adversarial review:
- `iron longsword` → `Broadsword`. The registry maps `longsword/greatsword/broadsword` to the Broadsword family. A Terraria modder typing "longsword" reasonably expects a heavy two-handed weapon. Corpus updated.
- `pickaxe-bladed greatsword` → `Broadsword`. Head noun is "greatsword"; "pickaxe-bladed" is a modifier. Codex agreed. Corpus updated.

**No registry change recommended.** Substring-trap precedence works correctly: shotgun beats gun, pickaxe beats axe, broadsword family beats sword, spear beats blade.

Codex's one **DISAGREE** verdict: prompt #19 `the moonlit echo` falls back to `Sword` with no keyword evidence — the fallback is arbitrary. Not a regression but a design observation. Document in spec; don't change behavior for the demo.

**Codex's corpus-level critique** (worth filing as follow-up, not blocking the demo):
- Corpus only covers `Weapon` content_type. No accessories, summoner staffs, yoyos, flails, throwing, vanity. "Claude Code for Terraria" positioning implies more.
- No misspellings or messy real-user phrasing.
- Add an unambiguous Pickaxe prompt with pickaxe head noun (separate from #21).

## Tier B — architect manifests (~$0.50, ~5 min)

Run: `python3 -m qa.run_qa --tier B` → 9/10 (only #1 "fail" is the corpus expectation correction above; all 10 manifests are schema-valid with stats in tier range).

**Claude (mine):** stats land in `TIER_TABLE` ranges; sub_types match expected; tooltips and visuals are coherent and item-specific (not generic templates); no vanilla-name collisions in the names.

**Codex adversarial review surfaced three real product issues, ranked by demo severity:**

### 1. **BLOCKER (silent dud in-game).** Ranged/magic sub_types have `mechanics.shoot_projectile: null`
4/10 manifests are mechanically non-functional:
- `#6 repeating crystal pistol` — `ammo_id: AmmoID.Bullet` set, `shoot_projectile: null` ❌
- `#7 obsidian shotgun` — same ❌
- `#9 verdant elven longbow` — `ammo_id: AmmoID.Arrow` set, `shoot_projectile: null` ❌
- `#11 frost staff` — tooltip "Casts jagged glacier shards", `combat_package: frost_shatter`, `shoot_projectile: null` ❌

If the LinkedIn demo records a viewer firing a generated gun and nothing comes out, the pitch breaks. **Either fix the architect to populate shoot_projectile for ranged sub_types, or restrict the demo to melee items (Sword/Broadsword/Spear/Pickaxe).**

### 2. **BUG (pickaxe can't mine).** `#15 obsidian pickaxe`
Manifest has `content_type: Weapon`, `type: Weapon`, `tool_stats: null`. The Pickaxe sub_type is treated as a weapon shape with damage/use_time, not as a tool. In-game the item would deal damage but couldn't break blocks.

### 3. **NOTE (templating drift).** Stat copy-paste within tier+sub_type pairs
- `#3 Storm Brand` and `#4 Dawnthunder Claymore` both have damage 58, use_time 18, knockback 6.5, crit 8 — identical Tier3 sword block.
- `#17 Runic Trident` and `#20 Bladed Spear` share damage 33, knockback 5.8, auto_reuse, on_hit_buff Weak.

Architect isn't differentiating items within the same tier+sub_type cell. Cosmetic for the demo (you only ship one item at a time), but a problem for variant generation in the workshop loop.

### Findings

#### **DEMO BLOCKER #1.** Storm Brand failed `silhouette_readability`
The README and dry-run checklist both promote Storm Brand as the safe opener. Tier C ran it once and the sprite gate caught a non-readable sword silhouette. Sprite generation has stochastic variance; a single failure isn't proof of a chronic bug, but **before recording, run Storm Brand 3–5 times to measure the gate failure rate.** If > 30%, switch the README/dry-run hero prompt to one that survives Tier C reliably (the obsidian shotgun, frost staff, and obsidian pickaxe in this run all passed).

#### **DEMO BLOCKER #2.** Verdant Elven Longbow failed `occupancy`
Same issue at a different gate. Bows tend to be tall and thin; the `occupancy` gate measures pixel-fill of the canvas. Either rephrase the bow prompt with explicit framing (e.g. "centered horizontally, large icon") or drop bow as a backup demo prompt.

#### **PRODUCT BUG.** `generation_status.json` final state stuck at "building 95%"
After the pipeline completed for `MagmafractPickaxe`, the orchestrator log shows `Status → ready` and `Pipeline complete`, but the on-disk `generation_status.json` ended at `{"status": "building", "stage_label": "Compilation successful. Finalizing...", "stage_pct": 95}`. The "ready" state was either overwritten by a later "building" status update or never flushed. **The TUI watches this file** — if a viewer reproduces the demo and the file gets stuck, the TUI will show "Compilation successful. Finalizing..." forever. This was independently flagged by codex's first adversarial review of the demo plan and remains unresolved.

#### **HARNESS NOTE.** Tier C run_qa.py reads status before final flush
3 of 5 prompts show `pipeline_status: building` in the harness output even though their sprites landed on disk. This is the same race as the product bug above; harness inherits the symptom. Recommend reading `manifest` from a separate persistence path (e.g. `forge_inject.json`) instead of `generation_status.json` for QA.
